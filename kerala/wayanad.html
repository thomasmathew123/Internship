WAYANADU

Wayanad is a beautiful district nestled in the Western Ghats of Kerala, known for its lush greenery, rolling hills, and cool climate. Rich in natural beauty and biodiversity, it is home to dense forests, wildlife sanctuaries, waterfalls, and spice plantations. Wayanad also holds historical and cultural significance, with ancient Edakkal Caves featuring prehistoric carvings and several tribal communities preserving unique traditions. Popular tourist attractions include Banasura Sagar Dam, Soochipara Waterfalls, Pookode Lake, and Chembra Peak. With its serene environment and adventurous trekking trails, Wayanad offers a perfect blend of nature, history, and culture.



BEST PLACES TO VISIT


EDAKKAL CAVES

Edakkal Caves, located in the Wayanad district of Kerala, are famous for their ancient rock carvings and pictorial writings that date back to around 6,000 BCE. Perched on Ambukuthi Hills, the caves are accessible by a steep trek and offer stunning views of the surrounding landscape. The carvings inside depict human figures, animals, symbols, and tools, providing valuable insights into prehistoric civilization. Edakkal is the only known place in South India with Stone Age carvings, making it a significant archaeological and tourist site.

LAKKADI VIEW POINT

Lakkidi Viewpoint, located in the Wayanad district of Kerala, is a popular tourist spot known for its breathtaking views of the lush green valleys and winding mountain roads below. Situated at an altitude of about 700 meters above sea level, it offers a panoramic view of the Western Ghats and is often covered in mist, adding to its charm. The viewpoint is also the gateway to Wayanad and is surrounded by dense forests and rich biodiversity. Its serene beauty and cool climate make it a perfect stop for nature lovers and photographers.

PHANTOM ROCK

Phantom Rock is a fascinating natural rock formation located near Ambalavayal in Wayanad, Kerala. Shaped like a human skull, the rock stands atop a hill and is a popular destination for trekking and photography. Surrounded by lush greenery and scenic landscapes, it offers a unique blend of natural beauty and geological wonder. The eerie resemblance to a phantom's face gives the site its name and adds to its mystique, making it a must-visit for nature enthusiasts and adventure seekers.
