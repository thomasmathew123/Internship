KOZHIKODE

Kozhikode, also known as Calicut, is a historic coastal city in the northern part of Kerala. Once a prominent port on the Malabar Coast, it was the landing site of Vasco da Gama in 1498, marking the beginning of European trade in India. Known for its vibrant culture, Kozhikode blends tradition and modernity, offering beautiful beaches, bustling markets, and rich culinary heritage, especially its famous Malabar cuisine. The city is also a hub of education and literature, with a legacy of scholars, writers, and cultural institutions. With its historical significance, scenic charm, and warm hospitality, Kozhikode holds a special place in Kerala’s identity.



BEST PLACES TO VISIT


KAPPAD

Kappad is a historic beach located near Kozhikode in Kerala, famous as the landing place of Portuguese explorer Vasco da Gama in 1498. This event marked the beginning of European influence in India. The beach, with its serene shoreline, rocky outcrops, and peaceful ambiance, is a favorite among history enthusiasts and tourists alike. A small stone monument at the site commemorates Vasco da Gama’s arrival. Kappad’s natural beauty and historical significance make it a unique destination blending heritage with coastal charm.

PERUVANNAMUZHI DAM

Peruvannamuzhi Dam, located near Kozhikode in Kerala, is a picturesque reservoir built across the Kuttiady River. Surrounded by lush green hills and dense forests, the dam is part of the Kuttiady irrigation project and also supports drinking water supply and hydroelectric power generation. It is a popular eco-tourism spot, offering boating facilities and a small wildlife sanctuary nearby. The scenic beauty and tranquil environment make Peruvannamuzhi a relaxing destination for nature lovers and tourists.

MANACHIRA

Mananchira is a historic and scenic square located in the heart of Kozhikode city, Kerala. It is centered around a large man-made freshwater pond originally built by King Mana Vikrama, the ruler of the Zamorin dynasty. Surrounded by a beautifully maintained park, traditional Kerala-style buildings, and lush gardens, Mananchira is a popular spot for relaxation and evening strolls. The area reflects the cultural heritage of Kozhikode and serves as a peaceful retreat amid the city's bustle.
