IDUKKI

Idukki is a scenic and mountainous district in the Indian state of Kerala, renowned for its lush greenery, wildlife, and hydroelectric power projects. Nestled in the Western Ghats, it is home to dense forests, misty hills, and picturesque valleys. Idukki is famous for the Idukki Arch Dam, one of Asia’s highest arch dams, and also houses the Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary and the hill station of Munnar. The district is a major producer of spices like cardamom and pepper. With its cool climate, natural beauty, and ecological richness, Idukki is a haven for nature lovers and adventure seekers alike.



BEST PLACES TO VISIT


IDUKKI DAM

Idukki Dam, located in the Idukki district of Kerala, is one of the highest arch dams in Asia. Built across the Periyar River between two granite hills, Kuravan and Kurathi, it stands at a height of 168.91 meters. This engineering marvel is part of the Idukki Hydroelectric Project, which plays a crucial role in providing power to the state. Surrounded by lush greenery and scenic landscapes, the dam is not only vital for electricity generation but also a popular tourist attraction.

MUNNAR

Munnar is a picturesque hill station located in the Idukki district of Kerala, known for its sprawling tea plantations, misty hills, and cool climate. Nestled at an altitude of around 1,600 meters above sea level, it was once the summer resort of the British in South India. Munnar’s natural beauty, with rolling hills, waterfalls, and wildlife sanctuaries, makes it a popular destination for nature lovers and honeymooners. The town is also home to rare flora like the Neelakurinji, which blooms once every 12 years.

VALARA WATERFALLS

Valara Waterfalls is a stunning natural attraction located along the Kochi–Dhanushkodi highway in Kerala, near Adimali in the Idukki district. Surrounded by dense forests and rich greenery, the waterfall cascades down from a great height, creating a breathtaking sight, especially during the monsoon season. It is part of a chain of waterfalls on the Deviyar River and offers a serene stop for travelers heading to Munnar. The lush surroundings and the sound of rushing water make Valara a peaceful and scenic spot for nature lovers and photographers.
